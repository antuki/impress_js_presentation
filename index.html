Context

« The patriarchal South had made white men the dominant group […] A woman’s status depended upon her father or husband, her economic role was that of a marriage alliance-maker before marriage and a homemaker after marriage, her sexual role was that of a chaste maiden or a faithful wife...and her ideal temperament was passive, docile, ignorant, and virtuous. »

The Southern Belle in the American Novel
Kathryn Lee Seidel

Stereotypical views in Maycomb

« Maycomb is a place where ladies bathed before noon, after their three o'clock naps, and by nightfall were like soft teacakes with frostings of sweat and sweet talcum.»
Harper Lee uses the characters of the novel to bring gender roles for young girls to light, providing voices on both sides of the argument

Scout, the tomboy

« A girl who behaves like a spirited or boisterous boy »

Oxford English Dictionary

  1. resembled a boy in appearance
  2. preferred shorts/jeans to dresses
  3. preferred traditionally boys’ activities (e.g. climbing trees, playing army)
  4. preferred playing with boys rather than girls
  5. engaged in rough and tumble play

Childhood Tomboyism and Adult Androgyny (1996)
Burn, O’Neil & Nederend

Miss Maudie, the independant woman

  1. has no husband and no child
  2. is not afraid to voice her beliefs
  3. helps Scout to stand up against gender prejudices and judgments about others.
